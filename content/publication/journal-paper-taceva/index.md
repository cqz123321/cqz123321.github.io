abstract: |
  Variations in sensing principles and structural dimensions across vision-based tactile sensors (VBTS) can lead to major performance differences and make direct comparison difficult.
  TacEva addresses this challenge with a comprehensive quantitative evaluation framework that defines key performance metrics for practical robotic scenarios and provides structured experimental pipelines for each metric.
  By applying TacEva to multiple VBTS designs, the framework delivers consistent, comparable quantitative indicators across multifunctional sensing dimensions.
  The results support sensor pre-selection for task-specific applications and provide performance-guided insights for future VBTS design optimization.
